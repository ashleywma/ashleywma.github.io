iRaL: morphable quadrotor

Undergraduate researcher on the morphable quadrotor platform in the Intelligent Robotics and Autonomy Lab (iRaL), Aerospace Engineering, University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

Lab & advisor

I worked with Professor Vasileios Tzoumas on simulation and hardware-adjacent tasks for a tilt-rotor quadrotor that targets full 6-DOF wrench authority, not just the four thrust axes of a conventional quad. Project context: morphable drone site, GitHub org, and Aero department write-up on simulators and hardware.

What I did

  • Helped engineer the ROS / Gazebo simulation stack (public codebase on GitHub) for a morphable quadrotor with full 6-DOF control: dynamic morphing, trajectory planning, and controller testing on Ubuntu.
  • Worked with controls and hardware teammates so simulated dynamics tracked real flight data across morphing configurations.
  • Soldered board-level components that fly on the vehicle.
  • CAD support for a flight-test rig used to exercise the drone safely.

Why morphing matters

A standard quadrotor only has four independent motor thrusts, so it is underactuated for arbitrary wrenches in the body frame. Our platform adds actuated rotor tilt so the thrust directions can change: that extra freedom is what enables the team to pursue full 6-DOF control and more aggressive maneuvers than a fixed-pitch quad.

Papers (Prof. Tzoumas & team)

Morphable-aerial work moves quickly; a good entry point is the workshop paper on MorphEUS: Morphable Omnidirectional Unmanned System (ICRA OCRAIM workshop). The journal manuscript MorphQuad: Morphable Quadrotor for Superhuman Maneuverability, Manipulation, and Resiliency is listed on Prof. Tzoumas's publications page
